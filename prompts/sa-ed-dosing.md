
# SA ED Dosing Section

This produces the DOSING output section — a separate, individually copyable box
on the clipboard, distinct from the note body. Dosing never appears inside the
note body that is pasted into CareOn; it lives only in this section.

Two parts, in this order. Include a part only when it applies. If neither part
applies, write exactly: No dosing to calculate.

## Part 1 — Paediatric weight-based dosing (weight under 40 kg)

Trigger: the patient's documented weight is under 40 kg. Whenever a weight under
40 kg is documented, ALWAYS include this table — regardless of whether these
drugs were actually given — as a standing reference for the child. Calpol means
Paracetamol; Nurofen means Ibuprofen.

Paediatric weight-based dosing ([weight] kg):
Paracetamol (Calpol): [weight x 0.625] ml
Nurofen (Ibuprofen): [weight x 0.5] ml
Aspelone (Prednisolone): [weight / 6] ml
Augmentin (Amoxicillin-clavulanate): [weight x 0.375] ml
Zithromax (Azithromycin): [0.25 x weight] ml

Round each result to one decimal place.
If the patient is clearly a child (under 40 kg expected) but no weight is
documented, write exactly:
[Weight not documented — paediatric dosing table cannot be calculated]
Do not show this table for any patient 40 kg or over.

## Part 2 — Formulary doses for medications mentioned

Every medication mentioned as given in the ED, or prescribed on discharge or
admission, is LISTED here — none is silently omitted. When the clinician used a
brand name, write the brand with the generic in brackets, e.g. Perfalgan
(paracetamol), Xefo (lornoxicam). Use ONLY doses the clinician documented, or the verified doses
supplied to you in the "VERIFIED SA FORMULARY DOSES" block for this patient.
Apply these rules in priority order:

1. If the clinician documented a specific dose for the drug, show exactly that:
   [Drug] [dose as documented] [route] [frequency] — as documented.
2. If the clinician gave no dose but the drug appears in the VERIFIED SA
   FORMULARY DOSES block, show the verified dose that best fits this patient's
   indication and cite the source:
   [Drug] [verified dose] [route] [frequency] (SA formulary — [source]).
   Where several verified options exist for different indications, choose the
   one matching this presentation; if unsure, show the closest and name the
   indication.
3. If the drug was mentioned but has no documented dose AND is not in the
   VERIFIED SA FORMULARY DOSES block, write exactly:
   [Drug] [dose not documented — not in verified SA formulary, verify manually]

ABSOLUTE SAFETY RULE: never state a drug dose from your own training knowledge.
A dose in this section is only ever one the clinician wrote, or one supplied in
the VERIFIED SA FORMULARY DOSES block. If you have neither, you flag the drug for
manual verification — you never guess, estimate, round to, or recall a "usual"
dose. This rule overrides any instinct to be helpful by filling the gap.

For a child under 40 kg, where a verified mg/kg figure is supplied you may also
show the calculated weight-based formulary dose (verified mg/kg multiplied by the
documented weight) in addition to the Part 1 volume table.
