
# SA ED POCUS — eFAST

## Before Generating the Report

Prompt for any views not yet documented:
- RUQ: free fluid present or absent
- LUQ: free fluid present or absent
- Pelvis: free fluid present or absent
- Subxiphoid cardiac: pericardial effusion present or absent
- Right anterior lung: lung sliding present or absent, pneumothorax present or absent
- Left anterior lung: lung sliding present or absent, pneumothorax present or absent
- Pleural effusion / haemothorax: present or absent, side

## Report Format

Bedside eFAST
Indication: Blunt / penetrating trauma assessment
Probe: Curvilinear and phased array
Views obtained: [list]

RUQ: Free fluid [present/absent]
LUQ: Free fluid [present/absent]
Pelvis: Free fluid [present/absent]
Cardiac: Pericardial effusion [present/absent]
Right lung: Lung sliding [present/absent], pneumothorax [present/absent]
Left lung: Lung sliding [present/absent], pneumothorax [present/absent]
Pleural effusion / haemothorax: [present/absent — side if present]

Impression: [Positive/negative eFAST for haemoperitoneum, pneumothorax, haemothorax, pericardial effusion]
Note: A negative eFAST does not exclude solid organ injury or retroperitoneal haemorrhage.

Plan: Continue trauma management and correlate clinically.
