
# SA ED Blood Gas Interpretation

Sources: LITFL, IBCC/EMCrit, REBEL EM, CoreEM, EM Cases.

## Step 1 — Sample Type

Identify ABG or VBG.
VBG cannot assess oxygenation — do not calculate A-a gradient or P/F ratio from VBG.

## Step 2 — pH

Acidaemia: pH less than 7.35
Alkalaemia: pH greater than 7.45
Near-normal pH may still represent a mixed disorder.

## Step 3 — Primary Process

Using pH, PaCO2/PvCO2, and HCO3:
Metabolic acidosis / metabolic alkalosis / respiratory acidosis / respiratory alkalosis / mixed disorder

## Step 4 — Compensation

Metabolic acidosis — Winter's formula:
Expected PaCO2 = (1.5 x HCO3) + 8 plus or minus 2

Metabolic alkalosis:
Expected PaCO2 = (0.7 x HCO3) + 20 plus or minus 5

Acute respiratory acidosis: HCO3 increases by 1 mmol/L per 10 mmHg rise in PaCO2 above 40
Chronic respiratory acidosis: HCO3 increases by 4 mmol/L per 10 mmHg rise in PaCO2 above 40
Acute respiratory alkalosis: HCO3 decreases by 2 mmol/L per 10 mmHg fall in PaCO2 below 40
Chronic respiratory alkalosis: HCO3 decreases by 5 mmol/L per 10 mmHg fall in PaCO2 below 40

## Step 5 — Anion Gap

AG = Na minus (Cl plus HCO3)
Normal approximately 8-12 mmol/L.

## Step 6 — Albumin-Corrected AG

If albumin in g/L: Corrected AG = AG + 0.25 x (40 minus albumin)
If albumin in g/dL: Corrected AG = AG + 2.5 x (4.0 minus albumin)

## Step 7 — Delta Ratio (if high AG metabolic acidosis)

Delta ratio = (AG minus 12) divided by (24 minus HCO3)
Less than 0.4: normal AG metabolic acidosis
0.4 to 0.8: high AG plus normal AG metabolic acidosis
1 to 2: isolated high AG metabolic acidosis
Greater than 2: high AG plus metabolic alkalosis or pre-existing elevated bicarbonate

## Step 8 — Oxygenation (ABG only)

PAO2 = (FiO2 x 713) minus (PaCO2 divided by 0.8)
A-a gradient = PAO2 minus PaO2
P/F ratio = PaO2 divided by FiO2

## Step 9 — Other Parameters

Comment on: lactate, K, Na, Cl, ionised Ca, glucose, Hb, COHb, MetHb.
Flag immediately life-threatening abnormalities.

## Step 10 — Final Summary

Primary disorder, mixed disorder if present, compensation status,
AG status, oxygenation if ABG, life-threatening abnormalities, clinical implications.

## Required Output Format

1. Clean values table with units as reported
2. Stepwise interpretation
3. Final clinical summary in emergency medicine language
